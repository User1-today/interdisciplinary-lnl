Timeline of Important Events

2011

The Initial Phase of the Syrian war

- The war started as non-violent protests during the Arab Spring, calling for democratic reforms from President Bashar al-Assad. - The government reacted with brutal crackdowns, which resulted in defections from the Syrian army and the establishment of the Free Syrian Army (FSA). - Syria plunged into civil war as opposition forces armed themselves against Assad's regime.

2012-2013

Escalation of the Syrian war

- The fighting worsened throughout Syria, with engagements in Aleppo, Homs, and Damascus. - Different opposition movements developed, some secular rebels, some Islamist militiamen. - Foreign actors joined the fray: - The U.S., Turkey, and Gulf Nations supported oppositional groups. - Assad was defended by Iran and Hezbollah. - It became sectarian and divided further by Sunni rebels opposed to Assad's largely Alawite government.

2014

Rise of ISIS

- ISIS (Islamic State of Iraq and Syria) became a powerful force, capturing Raqqa and most of eastern Syria. - The group proclaimed a caliphate with Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi. - ISIS perpetrated atrocities, mass killings, and terror attacks, which attracted international attention.

2014-2015

U.S. Led Intervention and Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant advances

- The U.S. and its allies (including the UK and France) bombed ISIS in Iraq and Syria. - ISIS kept on expanding, capturing Palmyra and other strategic towns. - Kurdish fighters (YPG and SDF) became prominent, fighting ISIS in northern Syria with U.S. backing.

2015

U.S. Intervention and Rebellion Offensives

- The U.S. increased assistance to moderate rebels, training and arming them. - Syrian government troops, supported by Hezbollah and Iran, carried out counterattacks. - Assad's position remained weak by late 2015, so Russia intervened.

2015-2016

Talks of peace (Vienna) and Russian intervention

- The Vienna Peace Talks of 2015 were meant to establish a diplomatic way out but hit a snag as a result of entrenched divisions. - Russia intervened through military action (September 2015), bombing in favor of Assad. - The intervention by Russia changed the fortunes in Assad's favor, with the recapture of key cities. - The Vienna Peace Talks of 2015 were meant to establish a diplomatic way out but hit a snag as a result of entrenched divisions. - Russia intervened through military action (September 2015), bombing in favor of Assad. - The intervention by Russia changed the fortunes in Assad's favor, with the recapture of key cities.
